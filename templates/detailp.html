Fertilizer Recommendation System

This system helps farmers make informed decisions about fertilizers by analyzing soil data, crop types, and weather conditions.

Nitrogen (N)

Nitrogen is crucial for plant growth and the creation of chlorophyll, aiding in the plant's overall metabolism.

Phosphorus (P)

Phosphorus helps store and transfer energy in plants, impacting growth and reproduction processes.

Humidity

Humidity levels affect plant transpiration rates and can influence nutrient uptake. Higher humidity can reduce water loss but may also increase the risk of fungal diseases.

Temperature

Temperature plays a significant role in enzyme activity, photosynthesis, and overall plant growth. Extreme temperatures can stress plants, reducing their ability to absorb nutrients.

Soil Moisture

Adequate soil moisture is necessary for nutrient transport within the plant. Insufficient moisture can lead to nutrient deficiencies, while excessive moisture can result in root rot and poor aeration.

Soil Type

Different soil types (sandy, loamy, clay) affect water retention, drainage, and nutrient availability. Understanding soil type is crucial for selecting the right fertilizers and irrigation strategies.
